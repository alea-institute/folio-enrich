# Order Granting Motion for Summary Judgment

IN THE UNITED STATES DISTRICT COURT
FOR THE SOUTHERN DISTRICT OF NEW YORK

PLAINTIFF STERLING CAPITAL GROUP,
vs.
DEFENDANT MERIDIAN PROPERTY MANAGEMENT, LLC.

ORDER GRANTING MOTION FOR SUMMARY JUDGMENT

This matter comes before the Court on plaintiff's motion for summary judgment. Having reviewed the complaint, answer, brief, memorandum of law, affidavit, declaration, stipulation, evidence, and exhibit filed by both parties, and having considered oral argument at the hearing, the Court rules as follows:

FINDINGS OF FACT

Plaintiff and defendant entered into a real estate purchase agreement for commercial real property. The agreement contained representations and warranties, an indemnification clause, a limitation of liability clause, and a termination clause. Defendant committed breach of contract by failing to satisfy the closing conditions and condition precedent clause. The material adverse effect provision was triggered when defendant failed to disclose material contract obligations and assumption of liabilities in the disclosure schedules.

The Court has personal jurisdiction and diversity jurisdiction. The plaintiff has established standing, and the claims are not barred by mootness or ripeness. The statute of limitations has not expired, and the statute of frauds does not apply because the agreement was reduced to writing.

CONCLUSIONS OF LAW

The burden of proof under the clear and convincing evidence standard has been met. Plaintiff has demonstrated breach of contract, breach of fiduciary duty, and negligence through e-discovery, document production, deposition testimony, interrogatories, requests for production, requests for admission, and expert report.

The Court considered res judicata, collateral estoppel, and stare decisis. The Court reviewed the motion to dismiss for failure to state a claim and motion to dismiss for lack of jurisdiction, both previously denied. The Court also considered the motion in limine to exclude evidence and motion in limine to exclude expert testimony, ruling that the evidence and testimony were admissible.

The defendant raised attorney-client privilege and attorney work product protection, but the privilege log was deficient and the protective order was violated. The Court reviewed the settlement agreement, the dispute resolution clause, and the arbitration clause, finding that the litigation pathway was properly pursued.

ORDER

IT IS HEREBY ORDERED that plaintiff's motion for summary judgment is GRANTED. Judgment is entered in favor of plaintiff. Defendant shall pay damages, attorney fees, and costs. The indemnification clause and specific performance clause are enforceable. The Court retains jurisdiction over post-trial motions and enforcement. This consent order supersedes all prior scheduling order and case management order provisions.

The entire agreement clause, severability clause, governing law clause, force majeure clause, warranty clause, and survival clause of the agreement remain in full force and effect.

SO ORDERED.
